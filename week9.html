Soft robotics

Interdisciplinary Project Winter Semester 2018/2019 Rhine-Waal University of Applied Sciences FabLab Kamp-Lintfort by Adriana Cabrera, Prof. Karsten Nebe, Prof. William Megill Faculty Enviroment and Energy

Usually, robots are made of hard material. But engineers were inspired by the octopus for example to build robots that are flexible and soft as the name says it.
Soft robotics may be used to build wearable devices, but also for surgery.

Air plays a role in soft robotis, too. It makes some object more squishy whereas when you take out the air, they are more stiff.
